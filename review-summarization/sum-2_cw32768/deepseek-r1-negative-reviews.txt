The fixed media responded with subtle layers of processed sounds—drones, echoes, and rhythmic pulses—that seemed to ebb and flow in time with the live instrument. There was an intricate dialogue between the organic warmth of the viola and the cold precision of the electronics, creating moments of tension and release that kept me engaged throughout.

As the piece progressed, the interplay between the two elements became more dynamic, with the fixed media taking center stage at times with sharp, percussive bursts or shimmering harmonies. The composer’s use of space and texture was masterful, leaving room for introspection while also introducing flashes of energy. Despite its complexity, there was a cohesion that tied everything together, making the journey through sound feel both intimate and expansive.

By the end, I felt as though I’d experienced something deeply personal and transformative—a blend of technical skill and emotional depth that left a lasting impression.
